What This Calculator Does

This calculator estimates how much salary you keep after U.S. federal taxes. It applies the standard deduction for the selected filing status, estimates federal income tax using progressive tax brackets, and optionally estimates Social Security and Medicare taxes.

The result is an estimated annual net figure plus monthly, bi-weekly, and weekly after-tax salary. It is designed for fast planning, job comparison, and salary reality checks rather than payroll processing.

Calculation Methodology

This calculator uses a deterministic federal-tax estimate flow. First, it applies the standard deduction tied to the selected filing status. Next, it calculates taxable income and applies progressive U.S. federal tax brackets to estimate federal income tax. If the FICA option is enabled, it also estimates Social Security tax up to the annual wage base and Medicare tax on wages.

Total estimated taxes are then subtracted from gross salary to produce estimated net annual pay. That net figure is converted into monthly, bi-weekly, and weekly estimates. All calculations run locally in the browser and no personal data is collected or stored.

Understanding After-Tax Salary Estimates

Gross salary and after-tax salary are not the same thing. A compensation figure shown in a job offer or raise discussion is usually the gross amount before federal tax, Social Security, and Medicare are considered. That is why a salary can look comfortable on paper but feel smaller once you translate it into the amount that actually reaches your bank account.

This calculator keeps the estimate intentionally narrow. It focuses on U.S. federal income tax and optional FICA so the output stays deterministic and easy to understand. State income taxes, pre-tax deductions, health insurance premiums, retirement contributions, tax credits, and employer-specific withholding choices can all move real paycheck amounts away from the estimate. The purpose of the tool is not to mimic payroll software line by line. The purpose is to provide a fast planning estimate that gives users a cleaner picture of what gross salary may become after core federal taxes.
